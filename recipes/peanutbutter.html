Peanut Butter Cookies

Description

Classic crisscross peanut butter cookies.

Recipe credit: Sally's Cookie Addiction
Photo credit: Me

Ingredients

  • 270g of all-purpose flour (中力粉)
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 120g of unsalted butter, softened to room temp
  • 95g of vegetable shortening, room temp
  • 225g of brown sugar
  • 200g of granulated sugar, divided
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 325g of creamy peanut butter
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, using a handheld mixer or stand mixer, fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the butter on medium-high speed until smooth, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the brown sugar and 100 grams of the granulated sugar, and beat on medium-high speed until creamed, about 2 minutes.
  4. Add the eggs and beat on high speed until combined, about 1 minute.
  5. Add the vanilla extract and peanut butter and beat on medium-high speed until combined.
  6. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix on low speed until combined.
  7. Cover and chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour (and up to 4 days).
    8. If chilling for more than 3 hours, allow to sit at room temp for at least 30 minutes before rolling and baking because the dough will be quite hard.
  8. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees C (160 if using a convection oven). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. Set aside.
  9. Scoop balls of dough, about 1 tablespoon of dough per cookie, then roll each in the remaining 100 grams of granulated sugar to coat. Place the dough balls 3 inches (7.5 cm) apart on the baking sheets. Press a fork into the tops to create the crisscross pattern.
  10. Bake for 15 to 17 minutes, or until lightly browned on the sides. The centers will look a little soft.
  11. Remove from the oven and allow to cool on the baking sheets for 5 mintues before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
