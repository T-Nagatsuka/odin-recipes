Oatmeal Carrot Cake Cookies

Description

Recipe credit: Sally's Cookie Addiction
Photo Credit: Me

Ingredients

  • 180g of all-purpose flour (中力粉)
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 240g of unsalted butter
  • 225g of brown sugar
  • 100g of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 20g of dark molasses (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 165g of freshly grated peeled carrots
  • 270g of old-fashioned whole rolled oats

Steps

  1. Whisk the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, cloves, ginger, nutmeg, and salt together in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, using a handheld mixer or stand mixer, fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the butter on medium-high speed until smooth, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the brown sugar and granulated sugar, and beat on medium-high speed until creamed, about 2 minutes.
  4. Add the eggs, molasses (if using), vanilla extract, and beat on high speed until combined, about 1 minute.
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix on low speed until combined.
  6. With the mixer running on low speed, beat in the carrots and oats.
    7. The dough will be thick and sticky.
  7. Cover and chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour (and up to 4 days).
    8. If chilling for more than 3 hours, allow to sit at room temp for at least 30 minutes before rolling and baking because the dough will be quite hard.
  8. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees C (160 if using a convection oven). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. Set aside.
  9. Scoop balls of dough, about 2 tablespoons of dough per cookie and place 3 inches (7.5 cm) apart on the baking sheets. Bake for 13 to 14 minutes, or until lightly browned on the sides. The centers will look a little soft.
  10. Remove from the oven and allow to cool on the baking sheets for 5 mintues before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
